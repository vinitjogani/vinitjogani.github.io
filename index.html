About me

I love building things and solving problems - the empowering nature of creation and the ability to translate the gravity of a figment of thought into something that achieves a certain goal or solves a certain problem has always fascinated me, which is espcially what drove me towards Computer Science. I believe that the beauty of being able to build meaningful experiences, intelligent products and ultimately world-changing technology is impeccable. It is my awe towards these endless possibilities that drives my purpose of life and I wish to someday extend the boundaries of those possibilities and bring a fruitful and positive change in the world.

I'm passionate about learning, because through past experiences as well as my upbringing in an education-intensive family, I have realized that what is learned is never wasted. A learned skill can manifest itself into different situations in the most unexpected of ways. After all, part of our breakthrough in Computer Science has been the realization that the breadth and scope of problems that learning can solve is much larger than what static inferential logic can.

Lastly, I also love art. The emotions that artworks (of various forms including visual, musical, theatrical, and written) can evoke in the audience have had a great influence on my worldview, and my exposure to art from a young age has shaped how I have grown as a person. Playing the guitar or the keyboard have lifted me up on the dullest days, and sometimes even reading a piece of poetry has changed my outlook on life. You can find some of my keyboard records on using the SoundCloud link in the links section.
