ABOUT CCSL.

The Canadian Conference on Student Leadership is a nationally recognized and respected annual conference that brings together post-secondary student leaders from across Canada to share ideas, become inspired, and leave determined to make a difference in their campuses and their communities. Delegates gain insight into leadership programs and experiences offered at other institutions, improve on their personal leadership skills, and are exposed to motivational life stories form highly renowned Canadian leaders.

The 2018 conference will be held at University of Toronto from November 23 to November 25. Mark your calendars!

MISSION.

CCSL celebrates the diversity of Canadian leadership programs, sharing a firm interest in the identity of national student leadership. It offers relevant and engaging programming + networking opportunities that are founded in student development and transformational learning principles. It further connects and engages professional staff in discussions on best practices and approaches to student leadership development.

The CCSL recognizes that the Canadian leadership community will become stronger and more effective through enhanced connectedness and structured opportunities to share and learn together, and it exists to develop a culture of purposeful leadership engagement that inspires change.

VISION.

The vision and legacy of the CCSL is achieved through a sustainable approach to conference programming, host bid/execution process and a large number of well-developed workshops facilitated by diverse student leaders and professional staff from across the country. Each year, the CCSL is held in a different part of the country, allowing for a new dynamic team of student volunteers to actively engage their peers in leadership learning. The uniqueness of each campus and province will ignite a new leadership theme and feel to the conference allowing for a cultural learning experience for delegates.
